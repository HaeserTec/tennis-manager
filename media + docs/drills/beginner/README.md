# Beginner Tennis Drills

## Overview
These drills are designed for absolute beginners to tennis, focusing on building fundamental skills, confidence, and enjoyment of the game. Sessions should be kept short and fun to maintain interest.

## Equipment Needed
- Tennis racket (suitable for beginners)
- Tennis balls
- Access to a tennis court or practice wall
- Water bottle and towel

## Warm-up (5-10 minutes)
- Light jogging around the court
- Arm circles and shoulder stretches
- Easy racket swings without balls

## Main Drills

### 1. Forehand Groundstrokes
- Stand in ready position with feet shoulder-width apart
- Practice hitting balls fed slowly from the coach or partner
- Focus on keeping the racket face open and following through
- Aim for consistent contact and ball going over the net

### 2. Backhand Groundstrokes
- Use both one-handed and two-handed grips as comfortable
- Start with slow feeds, emphasizing smooth swing
- Work on balance and footwork to move into position

### 3. Serve Practice
- Begin with underhand serves to build confidence
- Progress to overhand serves focusing on toss and contact
- Emphasize accuracy over power initially

## Key Principles
- Introduce basic stroke mechanics: forehand, backhand, and serves with slow feeds.
- Emphasize consistent ball contact, balanced stance, and controlled footwork.
- Keep session lengths short with frequent rest and coach feedback loops.

## Tips for Beginners
- Don't worry about power; focus on control and consistency
- Practice with a partner or coach for immediate feedback
- Have fun and celebrate small improvements
- Stay hydrated and take breaks as needed

## Progress Tracking
- Track progress in rally count, unforced errors, and confidence build-up.
- Keep a simple journal noting what went well and areas for improvement
- Aim to increase rally length gradually over sessions

## Cool-down
- Gentle stretching of major muscle groups
- Review what was learned in the session
- Positive reinforcement and encouragement
